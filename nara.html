Destination

Nara

Japan's First Great Capital

Where deer roam free among ancient temples

Nara was Japan's first permanent capital (710–794 AD) and remains one of its most enchanting cities. Most famously, over 1,200 sacred deer roam freely through Nara Park and the surrounding temple grounds — considered divine messengers in Shinto tradition.

The city is home to some of Japan's oldest and most significant Buddhist architecture, including Todai-ji, which houses the world's largest bronze Buddha statue in one of the world's largest wooden buildings. The scale is breathtaking even for seasoned travellers.

Nara is compact and walkable, making it an ideal day trip from Osaka or Kyoto. But those who stay overnight discover a quieter, more intimate side of Japan that the day-trippers miss entirely.

Best Time to Visit

Spring for cherry blossoms among the deer, Autumn for spectacular red and gold foliage around the temples. Summer features the atmospheric Obon lantern festival at Todai-ji.


Getting There

45 minutes from Osaka, 50 minutes from Kyoto by train. We include Nara in our Kansai multi-city itineraries with seamless transfers.

Must-See Highlights

What to experience in Nara

🐦

Nara Park & Deer

Feed and interact with the city's famous friendly deer. Buy special deer crackers (shika-senbei) from local vendors — the deer know to bow before receiving them.

⛩

Todai-ji Temple

Home to the 15-metre Great Buddha (Daibutsu), cast in 752 AD. The wooden hall that houses it is one of the largest timber structures ever built.

🌳

Kasuga Grand Shrine

A Shinto shrine famous for its thousands of stone and bronze lanterns, donated by worshippers over centuries. Magical when lit during festivals.

🍴

Naramachi

The old merchant quarter — narrow lanes lined with traditional wooden houses, now home to cafés, craft shops, and small museums.

🍁

Yoshikien Garden

A hidden gem with three distinct garden styles — moss, pond, and tea ceremony — offering peaceful beauty without the crowds.

🍵

Mochi & Local Cuisine

Try Nara's famous yomogi mochi (mugwort rice cakes), kakinoha-zushi (persimmon leaf sushi), and local craft beer at the growing brewery scene.
